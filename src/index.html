Analyzing Media Funding

Matt Nisbet and John Wihbey, Northeastern University
Design: Aleszu Bajak and Felippe Rodrigues

Despite the calls for increased funding and the many challenges facing media philanthropy, there has been limited data on scope and focus of non-profit funding.

Legacy funders of non-profit journalism that along with entrepreneurial investors have over the two decades helped create a growing and diverse ecosystem of national, local, deep vertical and advocacy-oriented news nonprofits. Provide estimates on #s and describe their unique digital native nature (API, Pew, Lewis).

These non-profits have joined a 50 year old infrastructure of public radio and public TV stations (various sources) and a century-old roster of advocacy, big ideas and public affairs magazines on the left, right, and in the center such as The Nation, Mother Jones, The Atlantic, Harpers, and National Review.

What national non-profit media grants are supporting

National non-profits and most local investigative units require distribution by other outlets which have platforms, readers, and reach. “That’s one of the real challenges. There’s nothing to sell on a day-to-day basis. News nonprofits are sort of journalism intermediaries.”

Report for America and GroundTruth Project subsidizing reporters at commercial newspapers / Center for Cooperative Media -- national and local news sharing / Propublica localization of stories by way of public databases.

But a Catch 22: Giving away content assures impact, but it also “locks them into” funding from foundations, the very foundations that have insisted they are not in it for the long term (Benson).
